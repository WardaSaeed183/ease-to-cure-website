Blood Sugar Level Chart

Condition Fasting (mg/dL) After Meal (mg/dL)
Normal 70–99 Less than 140
Prediabetes 100–125 140–199
Diabetes 126 or higher 200 or higher

Control Tips

  • Monitor blood sugar regularly
  • Eat balanced, low-carb meals
  • Exercise 30 mins daily

When to Seek Help

Seek emergency care if sugar exceeds 300 mg/dL or falls below 50 mg/dL, especially with symptoms like confusion or unconsciousness.

Causes of High Blood Sugar

  • Unhealthy diet
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Stress or illness
  • Skipping medication
  • Hormonal imbalances

Symptoms of High Blood Sugar

  • Increased thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow healing wounds

Preventive Lifestyle Changes

  • Maintain a healthy diet
  • Stay physically active
  • Regular health checkups
  • Manage stress levels
  • Avoid excess sugar and processed foods
